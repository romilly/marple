    Arithmetic Functions¶

    + Plus¶

    Monadic: Conjugate¶

    For real numbers, returns the argument unchanged (identity).

          +5
5
      +¯3
¯3

    Dyadic: Add¶

          3+4
7
      1 2 3+10 20 30
11 22 33
      100+⍳5
101 102 103 104 105

    - Minus¶

    Monadic: Negate¶

          -5
¯5
      -¯3
3
      -1 2 3
¯1 ¯2 ¯3

    Dyadic: Subtract¶

          10-3
7
      10 20 30-1 2 3
9 18 27

    × Times¶

    Monadic: Signum¶

    Returns ¯1, 0, or 1 depending on the sign of the argument.

          ×¯3
¯1
      ×0
0
      ×42
1

    Dyadic: Multiply¶

          3×4
12
      2×⍳5
2 4 6 8 10

    ÷ Divide¶

    Monadic: Reciprocal¶

    Returns 1÷⍵. Raises DOMAIN ERROR for zero.

          ÷4
0.25
      ÷2 4 5
0.5 0.25 0.2

    Dyadic: Divide¶

    Raises DOMAIN ERROR for division by zero.

          10÷4
2.5
      12÷1 2 3 4 6
12 6 4 3 2

    ⌈ Upstile¶

    Monadic: Ceiling¶

    Returns the smallest integer greater than or equal to the argument.

          ⌈3.7
4
      ⌈¯2.3
¯2
      ⌈5
5

    Dyadic: Maximum¶

          3⌈5
5
      1 5 3⌈4 2 6
4 5 6

    ⌊ Downstile¶

    Monadic: Floor¶

    Returns the largest integer less than or equal to the argument.

          ⌊3.7
3
      ⌊¯2.3
¯3

    Dyadic: Minimum¶

          3⌊5
3
      1 5 3⌊4 2 6
1 2 3

    * Star¶

    Monadic: Exponential¶

    Returns e raised to the power of the argument.

          *1
2.718281828
      *0
1

    Dyadic: Power¶

          2*8
256
      2*0 1 2 3 4
1 2 4 8 16

    ⍟ Log¶

    Monadic: Natural logarithm¶

          ⍟1
0
      ⍟*1
1

    Dyadic: Logarithm¶

    Left argument is the base.

          10⍟100
2
      2⍟8
3

    | Stile¶

    Monadic: Magnitude (absolute value)¶

          |¯5
5
      |3
3
      |¯1 2 ¯3
1 2 3

    Dyadic: Residue¶

    The remainder when dividing the right argument by the left. Note: APL residue has the left argument as the divisor (opposite to most languages).

          3|7
1
      5|12
2
      2|⍳6
1 0 1 0 1 0

    ○ Circle¶

    Monadic: Pi times¶

    Multiplies the argument by π.

          ○1
3.141592654
      ○0.5
1.570796327

    Dyadic: Circular functions¶

    The left argument selects a trigonometric or hyperbolic function:

    Left Function Left Function
    0 √(1-⍵²)
    1 sin ¯1 arcsin
    2 cos ¯2 arccos
    3 tan ¯3 arctan
    4 √(1+⍵²) ¯4 √(⍵²-1)
    5 sinh ¯5 arcsinh
    6 cosh ¯6 arccosh
    7 tanh ¯7 arctanh 
          1○○0.5           ⍝ sin(π/2) = 1
1
      2○0               ⍝ cos(0) = 1
1
      3○0               ⍝ tan(0) = 0
0
